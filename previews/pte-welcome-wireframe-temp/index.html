PTE Academic is a computer-based English test. You respond to text, audio, images and real-life situations by speaking, writing, choosing, arranging or typing.

Part 1 Speaking & Writing 76–84 minutes

Speak into a microphone, write on a keyboard and respond to written or recorded material.

Part 2 Reading 22–30 minutes

Choose words or answers, move text and show that you understand written meaning.

Part 3 Listening 31–39 minutes

Listen once, take notes when useful, then choose, type, highlight or write.

Your overall score is not the average of four skill scores. Some responses contribute to one communicative skill; others contribute to two.

How the computer test works

What happens on each screen

The exact controls and clocks change by task. Read the instruction first, then follow the screen in order.

Read
Check what response the task requires.
Prepare
Use any preparation countdown shown.
Watch or listen
Media may begin automatically.
Wait
For speaking, begin only when recording starts.
Respond
Speak, write, choose, move or type.
Move on
Check what you can, then select Next.
▶1

Recorded media plays once

Listening audio and video normally start automatically. Be ready before they begin. You may take notes.

●

Speaking is one recording

Wait for the tone or recording signal. Speak clearly before the time ends; you cannot replay and rerecord.

⏱

There are two kinds of clock

Some tasks have their own response time. Reading and much of Listening use a shared section clock.

½

Many tasks give partial credit

A partly correct response can still earn points. Exact marking depends on the task.

−

Three task types have penalty risk

Wrong selections can deduct points in both multiple-answer tasks and Highlight Incorrect Words.

R·W·L·S

Some responses reach two skills

For example, Repeat Sentence contributes to Listening and Speaking. This is not a fixed percentage weight.

Good test-day habits

Check the required response before you start.
Use the headset and microphone check carefully.
Keep an eye on the clock without staring at it.
Use notes to capture meaning, not every word.
Keep speaking until your response is complete.
For penalty tasks, select only what you can justify.
Trust the instruction on the screen. It tells you what to do, what is playing and how much time you have.

Your first three steps
01Confirm your target

Check the exact PTE version and score requirement with the institution or authority that will receive your result.

02Learn the real format

Use official task information and complete an official-format baseline under realistic conditions.

03Build from evidence

Use your actual errors and score profile to decide what to practise first. Do not guess from internet “weight” charts.
